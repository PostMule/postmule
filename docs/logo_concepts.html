PostMule — Logo Concepts

10 directions exploring clearer mule iconography. Each concept is shown at three sizes (large / medium / favicon) on both light and dark backgrounds. Brand palette only throughout. Concepts marked Recommended are strongest across all use cases.

Concept 01
Long Ears — Refined Front Face
Closest to the current mark. Ears are 2× taller relative to head, muzzle is now an explicit shape, nostrils added. Reads more clearly as a donkey/mule — the long-ear-to-head ratio is the signature feature. Head is elongated (taller than wide) which is distinctly mule, not cat or bear.

Most conservative evolution from current design. Preserves familiarity while fixing the "is it a mule?" ambiguity through proportion.

Concept 02
Letter Mule — Envelope as Face
The envelope IS the mule. Ears protrude from the top corners of the envelope shape, with eyes on the face and brass nostril dots. Instantly communicates both "mail" and "mule" without ambiguity. The envelope-flap becomes the snout line. Distinctive and memorable — no other service could use this mark.
Recommended Website Social media GitHub Letterhead

Strongest concept for recall. The metaphor is immediate — you see both the product (mail) and the brand (mule) in one shape. Works especially well on letterhead and social media where context reinforces the message.

Concept 03
Pack Mule — Full Body Carrying Letters
A mule in its natural role: carrying a load. The "load" is a letter/envelope strapped to its back with brass bindings. Full body profile view makes it unambiguously a mule (four legs, animal proportions). The short upright ear in profile distinguishes it from a horse. Narrative and playful.
Recommended Website hero About page Social media

Best storytelling logo. Immediately explains the product concept. Works well at large sizes but loses detail at favicon scale — best paired with a simpler favicon variant (Concept 6 or 9).

Concept 04
Profile Head — Short Upright Mane
Side profile of the head and neck. The short upright mane (three brass tufts along the crest) is the clearest visual distinction between a mule and a horse — horses have long flowing manes, mules have short upright ones. One long ear in profile. Elegant and clearly animal without being ambiguous about the species.
Letterhead Documentation Website

Most dignified and "financial-grade" option. Looks at home on a letterhead or report cover. Less fun than others, which may or may not suit the brand direction.

Concept 05
Postmark — Stamp/Seal Aesthetic
A traditional postmark stamp format: dashed circular border, mule face centered within. The dashed outer ring is universally read as "postal" or "official." Communicates authenticity and official document handling. The circular format is ideal for stamps, seals, and embossing on physical documents and letterhead.
Letterhead Official documents Social media Stamp/seal

Strongest option for print and official document contexts. The circular format works perfectly as a social media avatar and GitHub profile image. The stamp aesthetic reinforces the postal/mail handling concept.

Concept 06
Ear Mark — Ultra-Minimal
Three shapes: two tall ears, one circle. Reduces the mule to its single most identifying feature. Works at any scale — from 16px favicon to billboard. The asymmetric brass inner lining creates visual interest and color identity even at tiny sizes. Clean enough for embossing and single-color print.
Recommended Favicon App icon GitHub avatar Emboss/print

Best favicon and small-format option. The two tall ears are distinctive at any size. Should be paired with the wordmark for most contexts — works as standalone mark once brand recognition is established.

Concept 07
Peeking Mule — Eyes Over a Stack of Bills
A mule peeking over a stack of papers/bills. The "peeking" posture is immediately readable and charming. The bill lines on the papers (including a brass-colored amount line) add context. Communicates "PostMule handles your bills" in a single image. Highly differentiating — no other financial/mail tool looks like this.
Website hero Social media Marketing

Most "social media native" concept — the peering expression is engaging and shareable. Less suited for formal contexts (letterhead) but excellent for the public website and social profiles where personality matters.

Concept 08
Bold Block — Chunky Front Face
Same front-face concept but with much bolder, chunkier geometry. Thicker ears, larger eyes, prominent muzzle block. Inspired by Nounverse/Noun Project style: high contrast, no detail that disappears at small sizes. The wide muzzle block is specifically mule-like in proportion — horses have narrower faces.
App icon Social media Sticker/merch

Most recognizable mule face in the set due to the proportion emphasis on ears and muzzle. Works as an app icon or badge sticker. Slightly more casual in feel than other concepts.

Concept 09
Circle Badge — Social-Media-First
Mule face in a filled circle. The circle boundary crops the ears in a natural way that makes them even more prominent — they break out of the bounding circle creating energy. Two versions: navy-on-steel (default) and brass-circle (high-contrast dark variant). Purpose-built for GitHub avatar, Twitter/X profile, and Discord server icon.
Recommended GitHub avatar Social profiles App icon Favicon

Strongest option for all circular-crop formats. The brass variant (shown on dark side) is particularly striking — usable as a secondary/alternate mark for dark mode contexts or marketing materials.

Concept 10
PM Mark — Letterform with Ears
A "PM" monogram where each letter sprouts two mule ears from its uprights. Four brass-lined ears across the top of the letters. The letterform-based approach works well in typographic contexts — inline text, embossed on business cards, app headers. At favicon size, falls back to a pure mule face for clarity.
Business card Letterhead Emboss/stamp Inline text

Most typographic option. Works when the logo must sit inline with text or where the wordmark is redundant. Weakest at small sizes (favicon fallback to Concept 6 is recommended). Strongest as a standalone monogram on physical materials.
